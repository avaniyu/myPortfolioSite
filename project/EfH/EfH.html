Engineering for Humans

Interactive teaching tutorials on human perception, cognition and motor control theories. 

Engineering for Humans teaching tutorials provide students with approaches to the design and engineering of human-centred system, especially to learn how to design systems that increase productivity and that are safe to operate and robust to human error. The tutorials are aiming for students from all areas of technology, thus they are meant to be "black-boxes" that wrapping in-process computations and presenting resulting conclusions in tangible and understandable manner.

Keystroke-Level Model (KLM)

A predictive model on the time it takes for an expert user to accomplish a routine task without errors using an interactive computer system.

Consumed Endurance

A predictive model on the fatigue or consumed energy of a user from sustaining an interaction.

Fitts' Law

A predictive model of human movement, often used to model the act of pointing.

Hick-Hyman Law

A description on the time it takes for a person to make a decision as a result of the possible choices he or she has.

Multiple Resource Theory

A predictive model on the relative differences in task interference between different task configurations.
