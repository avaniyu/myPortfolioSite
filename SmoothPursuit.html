Smooth Pursuit

Eye tracking calibration for public displays.

Why calibration?

In public space like streets, bus stops, and tourist centres, public displays play advertisements to passerbys frequently in scrolling manner.

It's easy to mount webcams on public displays to sense audience interests on advertisement content by eye tracking, in order to target customers.

However, people have different heights, they glance at the displays and pass by quickly. To guarantee reasoable eye-tracking accuracy in this situation, the problem is...

How to make eye-tracking work for different people, in a short time?

Smooth Pursuit Calibration

Calibrate audience gaze on public displays in real-time and non-intrusively using smooth pursuit eye movement.

I pre-defined the front-end object moving trajectory driven by system-wise time, and streamed gaze point in backend using Tobii eye tracker, then compute the correlation of the movement patterns of this two streams by synchronising system-wise time. Once their correlation went above pre-defined threshold in certain time intervals, namely the gaze of test subject followed the moving object, I have this two streams within those time intervals as reference, to calibrate the following gaze using pinhole projection model and homography matrix H in OpenCV.
